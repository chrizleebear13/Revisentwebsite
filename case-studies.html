Building Lifelong Green Habits

La Cañada Flintridge's Crestview Preparatory School uses Revi to educate students on proper waste sorting while building sustainable habits from an early age.

K-12 Education La Cañada Flintridge
Read full story

Deployment at the 5th grade outdoor waste station

The Challenge

As a green-thinking independent school, Crestview Preparatory has long prioritized environmental education as part of their curriculum. However, administrators noticed a disconnect: while students learned about sustainability in the classroom, those lessons weren't translating to action at lunchtime. Younger students in particular seemed unsure about where different items belonged, and the school wanted more than just well-organized bins—they wanted a hands-on tool that would help children truly understand and internalize proper waste disposal habits that would stick with them for life.

The Solution

Crestview partnered with Revisent to deploy Revi devices in the 2nd grade and 5th grade wings. The device's real-time visual feedback instantly captured students' attention—when a child holds up a milk carton or apple core, Revi's LED lights guide them to the correct bin before they toss it. Teachers incorporated the devices into their environmental science lessons, using the dashboard to track how each wing was doing. The 5th graders embraced the responsibility, with some students volunteering as "Revi Ambassadors" to help younger kids at the bins. Meanwhile, the 2nd graders turned sorting into a game, celebrating each successful sort. Within just 30 days of deployment, the impact was clear: proper disposal had become second nature across both wings, with students confidently sorting their waste without hesitation.

The Results

6,000+ Monthly interactions
+15% Recycling Rate
100% Student engagement

From Zero to Sustainable

Jellypop's new headquarters needed a waste management solution from scratch—and chose to build it right with Revi from day one.

Startup Corporate Office Los Angeles
Read full story

Early deployment at Jellypop HQ

The Challenge

When Jellypop moved into their new Los Angeles headquarters, they faced a blank slate: no existing waste infrastructure, no sorting stations, and no established habits among employees. As a company that values sustainability, they didn't want to simply place generic bins around the office and hope for the best. They wanted to get waste management right from the start—building a culture of proper sorting before bad habits could form.

The Solution

Jellypop partnered with Revisent to deploy Revi devices at key waste stations throughout their new office. Rather than retrofitting an existing system, they integrated Revi into their office design from day one. The devices provided immediate guidance to employees unfamiliar with California's specific recycling requirements, turning every disposal into a learning moment. Within a week, proper sorting became second nature—employees quickly learned which items went where, and the office established sustainable habits that would have taken months to develop otherwise.

The Results

+56% Increase in accurate sorting
1 week To full adoption
Zero Contamination incidents
